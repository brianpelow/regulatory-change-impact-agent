PROPOSED RULE ON AUTOMATED DECISIONING IN CONSUMER FINANCIAL SERVICES
Reference: ILLUSTRATIVE-2026-02
Comment period: 60 days from publication

PART A. APPLICABILITY

This part applies to any automated decision that determines a consumer's eligibility for, or the terms of, a consumer financial product.

PART B. NOTICE OF ADVERSE ACTION

(1) A covered institution must provide a statement of reasons to any consumer who receives an adverse action determined in whole or in part by an automated decision system.

(2) The statement of reasons shall identify the specific principal reasons for the adverse action. A generic reference to a proprietary scoring methodology is not sufficient.

(3) A covered institution is required to maintain a mapping between model outputs and the reason codes disclosed to consumers, and must retain that mapping for the life of the disclosure obligation.

PART C. FAIR LENDING AND DISPARATE IMPACT

(1) A covered institution must conduct testing for disparate impact across prohibited bases prior to deploying an automated decision system and periodically thereafter.

(2) Where testing identifies a disparity, the institution shall document its analysis of whether a less discriminatory alternative is available.

(3) A covered institution is required to monitor deployed systems for emergent disparities on a protected class basis and must establish thresholds that trigger investigation.

(4) Records of such testing and monitoring shall be retained and produced upon request.

PART D. RECORDKEEPING

(1) A covered institution must retain, for each automated decision, the input data considered, the model version applied, and the resulting determination.

(2) Such records shall be maintained in a form that permits reconstruction of the decision.

PART E. HUMAN REVIEW

(1) A covered institution must provide a consumer with a means to contest an automated decision and obtain review by a natural person.

(2) The institution shall not condition that review on the consumer waiving any right.
