GUIDANCE ON THIRD-PARTY ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SERVICES
Reference: ILLUSTRATIVE-2026-03

SECTION 1. PURPOSE

This guidance addresses institutional use of artificial intelligence capabilities supplied by third parties, including hosted model services and vendor-embedded automated decisioning.

SECTION 2. VENDOR DUE DILIGENCE

2.1 An institution must perform due diligence on any third party supplying an artificial intelligence capability that informs a consequential decision, and shall document the results of that assessment.

2.2 The assessment is required to address the vendor's model governance practices, its incident notification commitments, and its ability to support the institution's own recordkeeping obligations.

2.3 An institution shall not rely on a vendor attestation in place of its own evaluation where the capability informs a consequential decision.

SECTION 3. CONTRACTUAL PROVISIONS

3.1 Contracts with third-party providers must secure the institution's right to obtain records sufficient to reconstruct decisions produced by the vendor's system.

3.2 Such contracts shall require the vendor to notify the institution of material changes to the model or its operating parameters prior to those changes taking effect.

SECTION 4. ONGOING MONITORING

4.1 An institution is required to monitor the performance of third-party artificial intelligence capabilities on an ongoing basis.

4.2 The institution must establish criteria for escalation where vendor performance degrades or where the vendor fails to meet its notification commitments.

SECTION 5. INCIDENT NOTIFICATION

5.1 An institution must notify its primary supervisor of any incident involving a third-party artificial intelligence capability that materially affects consumers or the institution's operations.

5.2 Notification shall occur within the timeframe established by applicable reporting requirements, and the institution is required to track and evidence its compliance with that timeframe.

5.3 The institution shall retain an audit trail of each incident and the actions taken in response.

SECTION 6. CHANGE MANAGEMENT

6.1 A vendor-initiated change to a production capability must be subject to the institution's own change authorization process before it takes effect in the institution's environment.
